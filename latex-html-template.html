Assessing the Impact of the Fukushima Disaster on the Income Gap between Male and Female Workers

Abstract

This study examines the impact of the Fukushima disaster on the income gap between male and female workers in Fukushima Prefecture. Using a difference-in-differences (DiD) model and event study models, we find that the disaster had a disproportionate impact on female workers, leading to a decrease in their average income initially. However, as non-regular workers left the workforce, the average income of the remaining female workers increased. We also find that the disaster led to changes in the types of industries and occupations that female workers were employed in, with a shift towards higher-paying industries. Our results suggest that the disaster had a complex impact on the income gap between male and female workers, and that policies aimed at supporting female workers in the affected areas may be necessary to mitigate the negative effects of the disaster.
