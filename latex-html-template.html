Unified Emissions Market: A Synergistic Approach to Pollution Reduction

Abstract

This report proposes a multi-layered structure for unifying emissions markets, carbon markets, pollution emission markets, and energy rights trading markets. The framework is based on the concept of a 'cap-and-trade' system and aims to minimize costs through the efficient allocation of emissions allowances and the integration of energy rights trading. We discuss the application of Coase's theorem, Pigouvian taxes, and game theory to the unified emissions market, and explore the potential impact of market size on the effectiveness of policy interventions.

Introduction

The synergistic control of pollution and reduction is a critical challenge in environmental economics. This report proposes a multi-layered structure for unifying emissions markets, carbon markets, pollution emission markets, and energy rights trading markets. The framework is based on the concept of a 'cap-and-trade' system and aims to minimize costs through the efficient allocation of emissions allowances and the integration of energy rights trading.

Methods

The proposed framework consists of seven layers:

  1. Unified Emissions Registry: a centralized registry to track and record emissions from various sources.
  2. Emissions Allowance Allocation: allocation of emissions allowances to participating entities based on their historical emissions, industry benchmarks, or other relevant factors.
  3. Market Segmentation: creation of separate market segments for different types of emissions to account for varying abatement costs and environmental impacts.
  4. Inter-Market Trading: allowance for trading between market segments to facilitate the most cost-effective emissions reductions.
  5. Energy Rights Trading Integration: integration of the energy rights trading market into the unified structure.
  6. Cost Minimization Mechanisms: implementation of mechanisms to minimize costs, such as auctions, price floors and ceilings, and banking and borrowing provisions.
  7. Governance and Oversight: establishment of a governing body to oversee the unified market and ensure compliance.

Results

The proposed framework is expected to lead to a more efficient and effective emissions reduction strategy. The integration of energy rights trading and the use of Pigouvian taxes are expected to further optimize emissions reduction outcomes.

Discussion

The proposed framework is expected to lead to a more efficient and effective emissions reduction strategy. However, the impact of market size on the effectiveness of policy interventions is still unclear. Further research is needed to explore the application of game theory and the concept of Nash equilibrium to the unified emissions market.

The concept of Coase's theorem suggests that the initial allocation of property rights does not affect the efficiency of the market outcome. However, the application of Coase's theorem to the unified emissions market is still unclear.
