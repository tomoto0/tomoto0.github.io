The Impact of the 2011 Fukushima Earthquake on the Labor Market and the Gender Gap

Abstract

This study examines the impact of the 2011 Fukushima earthquake on the labor market and the gender gap in Japan. Using a combination of difference-in-differences and event study models, we find that the earthquake had a significant impact on the income gap between female and male workers. The results suggest that female workers experienced a larger decline in income compared to male workers after the earthquake. However, in the long term, female workers in Fukushima Prefecture experienced an increase in income due to migration to other prefectures and the influx of low-paying labor.
